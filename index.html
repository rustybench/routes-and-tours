Moments on the Trail

Common Questions

What you need to know before we walk.

What should I pack?

Sturdy hiking shoes, a hat, sun protection, and at least 2 liters of water. For gorge and coastal walks, I always recommend bringing a swimsuit.

Do I need to be an expert hiker?

Not at all. I offer routes for all levels, from easy village strolls to challenging scrambles. We'll pick a path that fits your experience.

How do we get to Evia?

Evia is connected to the mainland by two bridges at Chalkida (about a 1-hour drive from Athens). We can arrange a meeting point depending on the chosen route.

What if the weather is bad?

Safety comes first. If there's heavy rain or extreme heat, we'll either adjust the route to something shaded and safe, or reschedule entirely.

About Evia

The island of two faces.

Despite being Greece's second-largest island, Evia remains fiercely independent and untamed by mass tourism. It is an island of dramatic dualities: rugged alpine mountains plunge violently into the deep blue Aegean in the east, while the west coast yields to calm gulf waters and gentle, rolling foothills.

From the ancient petrified forest to the cascading waterfalls of Drimona and the alpine lakes of Mount Dirfys, every trail tells a different story. Just an hour's drive from Athens, yet a world away.

Terrain Alpine mountains, deep gorges, and wild Aegean coastline.
Highlights Mt. Dirfys, Dimosari Gorge, hidden pristine beaches.
Vibe Untamed, authentic, and traditional.
